About Me

I started my journey into data science as an engineering researcher at the University of Toronto—using streaming Internet of Things (IoT) location data, I modeled and predicted vehicle travel times for the entire Toronto transit system, a large urban network with over 44 thousand daily vehicle trips, in R and C#. Through my research, I discovered the innovative fields of data mining and machine learning. Seeing the power of everyday data to challenge traditional assumptions and to answer important questions compelled me to pursue a career in data science.

I have applied my data science toolkit, from data collection and feature extraction to visualization and predictive analytics, to enhance product features across product and service offerings at a large international organization. I used powerful data exploratory tools such as data.table in R and pandas in Python to perform data wrangling and extract hidden insight to inform business operations. I have introduced cutting-edge packages and tools to analyze and understand complex datasets.

I am passionate about advancing my skills in working with big data and building complex machine learning pipelines to create business value. Ultimately, I want to improve the products and services provided to my clients and customers.
